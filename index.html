About Me

I am an undergraduate Geography student with a deep academic interest in wildlife research, forest landscapes, and the spatial dynamics of natural environments. I am particularly fascinated by how wildlife habitats, protected areas, and human activities interact across space and time.

I am actively learning and practicing Geographic Information Systems (GIS), with a focus on QGIS and ArcGIS. My work involves land use and land cover analysis, basic habitat mapping, and spatial interpretation using satellite imagery and field-based data.

My long-term interest lies in applying GIS tools to wildlife conservation, habitat assessment, and environmental research, especially within forested and protected landscapes.

What I'm Looking For

I am seeking internships, learning opportunities, and field-based projects related to wildlife research, conservation studies, GIS applications, and environmental analysis.

I am especially interested in projects involving forest ecosystems, protected areas, wildlife habitats, land use change, and the application of QGIS and ArcGIS for spatial mapping and research support.
